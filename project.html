Clean water and sanitation refer to the availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation services for all, a goal recognized as a fundamental human right essential for health, dignity, and economic development. Achieving this involves providing access to safe drinking water, sanitation facilities like toilets, and promoting good hygiene practices to prevent diseases, as billions worldwide still lack these basics. Addressing clean water and sanitation challenges is crucial for poverty reduction, economic growth, and environmental sustainability, and is a key part of the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDG 6).
